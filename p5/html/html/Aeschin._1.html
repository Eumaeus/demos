This is a speech that Aeschines composed and delivered in a trial before a jury. In 346 BCE, Aeschines and Demosthenes served together on an embassy to Philip of Macedon. The subject of the embassy was an alliance between Athens and Philip, and protection for Phocis, an ally of Athens that was at war with Thebes. The embassy was not a success, and in 345 BCE Demosthenes and Timarchus brought charges of treason against Aeschines, claiming that he had been bribed to betray Athenian interests to Philip (Source for date: E. M. Harris, Aeschines and Athenian Politics [Oxford, 1995]). In order to delay his own treason trial, Aeschines brought charges against Timarchus, alleging that he had been a prostitute, which was illegal for an Athenian Citizen. Demosthenes defended Timarchus (that speech is lost), but Aeschines won his case.
