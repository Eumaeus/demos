1. Aristotle

384-322 BCE (source: OCD3). A philosopher, originally from Stagira in Chalcidice, who spent much of his career in Athens. He was a student of Plato for a time, then tutor to Philip of Macedon’s son Alexander (who would later rule in Macedonia as Alexander III and come to be called ‘Alexander the Great’), then, upon returning to Athens, the founder of a school of philosophy at the Lyceum. Aristotle’s works are diverse, including writing in philosophy, natural science, theology, and politics. (see philosophy).
