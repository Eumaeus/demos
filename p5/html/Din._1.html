1. Din. 1

(Dinarchus’ Against Demosthenes; see also Oratory). This is a speech written by Dinarchus for client (whose name we do not know) prosecuting Demosthenes in a case involving bribery. In 324 BCE, Harpalus, who was Alexander the Great’s treasurer, fled from Babylon and came to Athens with a large amount of Alexander’s money. He was admitted into the city, taken into custody with the money, but later escaped. Afterwards, it was found that several hundred talents was missing from the money he had brought to the city. The Areopagus investigated the matter, and issued a report, or apophasis (ἀποφάσις), saying that certain Athenians had accepted bribes from Harpalus. Demosthenes was named in the report. Source: Ian Worthington, A Historical Commentary on Dinarchus: Rhetoric and Conspiracy in Late Fourth-Century Athens (University of Michigan Press, Ann Arbor, 1992).
